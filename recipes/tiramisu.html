Tiramisu

Tiramisu is a beloved Italian dessert that combines bold coffee flavor with creamy, velvety layers. It starts with ladyfingers (savoiardi) dipped briefly in strong espresso or coffee, sometimes spiked with a splash of coffee liqueur like Kahlúa or Marsala wine. These soaked biscuits are layered in a dish with a smooth mixture of mascarpone cheese, egg yolks, and sugar, often lightened with whipped cream or beaten egg whites for a fluffier texture.

Once the layers are assembled—typically starting and ending with the mascarpone mixture—the tiramisu is refrigerated for several hours or overnight. This allows the flavors to meld and the dessert to set properly. Just before serving, it's generously dusted with unsweetened cocoa powder for a slightly bitter contrast to the sweet and creamy filling. The result is a luxurious, no-bake dessert that's rich, flavorful, and irresistibly indulgent.

Ingredients

  • Ladyfingers (savoiardi) – 24 to 30 pieces (enough to fill your dish in 2 layers)
  • Espresso or strong brewed coffee – 1 ½ cups (cooled)
  • Marsala wine or coffee liqueur (optional) – 2 tbsp
  • Mascarpone cheese – 1 lb (about 450g)
  • Heavy cream – 1 cup (whipped to soft peaks)
  • Egg yolks – 6 large (or use pasteurized egg yolks if you're concerned about raw eggs)
  • Granulated sugar – ½ cup
  • Vanilla extract – 1 tsp
  • Unsweetened cocoa powder – for dusting (about 2 tbsp)
  • Dark chocolate (optional) – grated or shaved for garnish
