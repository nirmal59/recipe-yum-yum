Full Meat Shawarma

Shawarma is a flavorful Middle Eastern street food made from marinated meat, usually lamb, chicken, or beef, that’s grilled or roasted on a vertical spit. To make the shawarma marinade, mix yogurt, olive oil, lemon juice, and a variety of spices like cumin, paprika, turmeric, garlic powder, cinnamon, and allspice. The combination of these spices gives the meat its signature savory, tangy, and aromatic flavor. Once your marinade is ready, coat the meat (cut into strips or chunks) and let it marinate for several hours or overnight to really absorb the flavors.

For cooking, you can either grill the marinated meat on skewers or pan-fry it until it’s golden brown and crispy on the edges. Serve the cooked meat in pita bread or flatbreads with toppings like fresh vegetables (tomato, cucumber, onions), lettuce, and a drizzle of garlic sauce or tahini. You can also add pickles and fresh herbs like parsley or mint for extra zing. Shawarma is perfect for a hearty sandwich or wrap, and it’s guaranteed to satisfy with its rich, spiced flavor!

Ingredients

  • Boneless chicken thighs, beef, or lamb – 1 ½ lbs (about 700g)
  • Yogurt – ½ cup (for tenderness and flavor)
  • Olive oil – 2 tbsp
  • Lemon juice – 2 tbsp
  • Garlic – 4 cloves (minced)
  • Ground paprika – 1 tsp
  • Ground turmeric – 1 tsp
  • Ground cinnamon – ½ tsp
  • Ground coriander – 1 tsp
  • Ground allspice – ½ tsp
  • Ground black pepper – ½ tsp
  • Salt – 1 tsp
  • Cayenne pepper (optional) – ¼ tsp (for some heat)
